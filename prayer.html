Fasting & Prayer

“But when you pray… and when you fast… your Father who sees in secret will reward you.” — Matthew 6:6, 18

The Heart of Biblical Fasting & Prayer

Jesus Himself fasted forty days and nights in the wilderness, drawing close to the Father and overcoming temptation (Matthew 4:1-11). He taught that certain breakthroughs come only “by prayer and fasting” (Matthew 17:21). This sacred discipline turns our hunger toward heaven, quiets the flesh, and opens our spirit to hear God’s voice more clearly.

“Come near to God and He will come near to you.” — James 4:8

Beautiful Blessings from Scripture

  • Deeper intimacy with Jesus and sensitivity to the Holy Spirit
  • Clarity and divine guidance for decisions (Acts 13:2-3; 14:23)
  • Humility before God, breaking pride and strongholds
  • Power over temptation and spiritual warfare (Matthew 4; Ephesians 6:12)
  • Renewed dependence on God’s Word alone (“Man shall not live by bread alone” — Matthew 4:4)
  • Answered prayer with urgency and faith (Ezra 8:23; Mark 9:29)
  • Secret reward from our loving Father who sees what is hidden (Matthew 6:16-18)

Simple Biblical Guidance

Begin with sincere heart — confess sins, set your intention to seek Jesus. Start small: skip one meal, or fast one day. Let every hunger pang become a call to pray, read the Word, or worship. Do it privately, not for show (Matthew 6:16-18). Hydrate well, break the fast gently with light food, and give thanks. Keep your eyes on Christ — this is about relationship, not ritual.

“Is not this the fast that I choose: to loose the bonds of wickedness… and not to hide yourself from your own flesh?” — Isaiah 58:6-7
